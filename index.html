‚ÑπÔ∏è About Construction-to-Permanent Loans

üí°
How It Works:
A construction-to-permanent (CTP) loan combines the construction loan and permanent mortgage into a single loan with one or two closings. During construction, you typically make interest-only payments on drawn funds. After construction completes and you receive the Certificate of Occupancy, the loan converts to a standard amortizing mortgage. Some lenders offer "single-close" products; others require a modification or second closing.

What Does a General Contractor Do? Click to expand

A GC manages the entire construction process. Here's what they handle:

üìÖ Project Management

  • Create and maintain construction schedule
  • Coordinate all subcontractors
  • Sequence work (foundation ‚Üí framing ‚Üí MEP ‚Üí finishes)
  • Handle schedule conflicts and delays
  • Manage material deliveries and staging

üìû Subcontractor Coordination

  • Hire and vet all subs (plumber, electrician, HVAC, etc.)
  • Negotiate pricing and contracts
  • Schedule inspections between trades
  • Ensure work meets code and specs
  • Handle disputes and quality issues

üí∞ Financial Management

  • Manage construction budget
  • Process draw requests to lender
  • Pay subcontractors
  • Track change orders
  • Maintain lien waivers

üìã Administrative

  • Pull permits and schedule inspections
  • Maintain insurance and bonding
  • Handle code compliance
  • Manage punch list at completion
  • Provide warranties
Can You Be an Owner-Builder?
Yes, but consider:
  • Pros: Save 10-20% GC fee, direct control, learn the process
  • Cons: Full-time job during build (6-12 months), need to know all trades' work, scheduling is complex, subs may charge more or prioritize GC jobs, lender may require more oversight
  • Middle ground: Hire a "construction manager" at 5-8% to advise while you do legwork
Owner-Builder Financing is Very Limited
Most construction lenders require a licensed GC, especially with less than 20% down. Here's why:
  • Risk to lender: Owner-builders have higher failure/delay rates
  • No warranty: GCs provide workmanship warranties; owner-builders don't
  • Draw management: Lenders trust GCs to manage funds properly
  • Insurance gaps: Builder's risk policies may not cover owner-builders

Bottom line: If you need financing (especially <20% down), plan on hiring a GC. Owner-builder typically only works with cash builds or very high equity positions (40%+).

üî® Construction-to-Permanent Loan Process

1

Pre-Qualification (1-2 weeks)

Submit financial documents, credit check, income verification. Get pre-qualified amount.

2

Builder Selection & Approval (2-4 weeks)

Select licensed contractor. Lender reviews builder credentials, license, insurance, and financials.

3

Plans, Specs & Contract

Submit finalized architectural drawings, specifications, and fixed-price construction contract.

4

Appraisal

"Subject to completion" appraisal based on plans. Confirms projected value.

5

Underwriting & Closing (4-6 weeks)

Full underwriting. Single close (or first of two). Rate may lock or float during construction.

6

Construction Phase (6-9 months)

Draw requests as milestones completed. Inspections before each draw. Interest-only on drawn funds.

7

Conversion to Permanent Mortgage

After C.O., loan converts. Some products require modification closing; others are automatic.
